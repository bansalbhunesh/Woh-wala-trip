# Product Requirements Document — Yaarlore

## Core Product Vision

Yaarlore (internal repo: `woh-wala-trip`) is a **cinematic AI trip documentary platform** designed for Indian friend groups. It transforms raw group travel photos into a fully produced documentary experience: a narrative arc, character roles, "chaos scores," era chapters, and shareable story pages — all generated by AI.

The core thesis: your trip already happened. The photos exist. Yaarlore turns them into something your group will talk about for years — a piece of mythology about who you all are together.

**India-first** is a deliberate design constraint, not just a market choice. Hinglish tone, Razorpay payments, WhatsApp-first sharing, and group-travel cultural patterns (the 4–8 person friend-group trip) are all first-class design assumptions.

---

## User Problems Solved

1. **The photo dump problem.** Every trip produces 300 photos that nobody looks at. Yaarlore turns that chaos into a story.
2. **The "who were we on that trip?" problem.** AI assigns character roles to each member based on actual behavior in photos. Your friends finally have documentary-grade roasts of each other.
3. **The "nothing to share" problem.** Most travel content is forgettable. A cinematic documentary with a chaos score of 84/100 and the verdict "Historically Cooked" is inherently shareable.
4. **The group memory problem.** After 5 trips, Yaarlore builds a longitudinal behavioral record of your friend group — who you each are, how you've changed, what recurring incidents define you.

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Indian college students and young professionals (22–32) who take group trips with the same friend group multiple times per year. Typically 4–8 people. Already sharing photos on WhatsApp.

**Secondary:** Anyone with a shared photo archive from a group trip who wants it transformed into something more than a Google Photos album.

---

## Product Philosophy

- **Cinematic documentary, not scrapbook.** Every design decision asks: does this feel like a HBO documentary about your friend group, or does it feel like a photo album?
- **The lore is earned.** You need ≥5 photos to generate. The product deliberately withholds output until there's enough signal to produce something real.
- **Chaos is the unit of value.** The "chaos score" (0–100, verdict tiers from "Mildly Simmering" to "Historically Cooked") is the metric users care about. It's shareable, rankable, and defensible.
- **Group mythology compounds.** The platform's moat is not the single-trip output — it's the longitudinal record across trips. After 5 years, Yaarlore holds the documented behavioral history of your specific friend group relationships.

## User Journeys

### Onboarding / First Trip

1. User arrives at landing (often via WhatsApp share link from a friend)
2. Sees public showcase of top-chaos trips → social proof
3. Signs up via OTP email (anti-spam: disposable domain blocklist + fraud scoring)
4. Creates first trip: name, destination, dates
5. Gets a shareable invite code → sends to group via WhatsApp
6. Group members join by entering invite code at `/trips/join`
7. Upload photos (min 5, free tier cap 50 photos / 500 MB)
8. Creator clicks "Generate Lore"

### Lore Generation

1. System validates ≥5 photos, user owns trip, no concurrent generation running
2. Monthly token cap checked (first generation always free; referral bonus also bypasses)
3. Atomic DB claim (`claim_lore_generation` RPC, Postgres `FOR UPDATE`)
4. HTTP trigger sent to AI worker (FastAPI on Render) with HMAC signature
5. Worker runs 8-step pipeline (see AI_PIPELINES.md for full detail)
6. User sees generating page — Supabase Realtime channel polls `lore_pipeline_state` per-step
7. Pipeline complete → `lore_status = 'ready'` → user redirected to story view
8. If HTTP trigger fails (cold start), generation_jobs queue catches it within 60s

### Story Experience

1. Trip page shows: chaos score, cooked verdict, tagline, full narrative, eras
2. Each member has a character role card (title, archetype, chaos rating)
3. Receipt stats (cinematic "trip stats" like a Spotify Wrapped)
4. Trip cover art (fal.ai Sana Sprint generated)
5. Shareable via public `/t/[code]/story` URL

### Sharing / Virality

1. Creator can toggle `story_visible` to make story public/private
2. OG card generated by Satori edge renderer at `/api/card/[tripId]`
3. WhatsApp share pre-fill via `wa.me/?text=` with story URL
4. Anniversary email at 1 year; first-week follow-up email at 7 days post-lore

### Battle Flow

1. User challenges another trip (must both have `lore_status = 'ready'`)
2. Battle created in `trip_vs_trip` table with 48-hour voting window
3. All members of both trips see `battle_started` pulse event on home feed
4. `judge_battle` background job runs AI verdict (Claude Sonnet)
5. Authenticated votes cast via `cast_vs_vote` RPC (server-authoritative dedup)
6. Result shown on `/battles/[battleId]`

### Payment / Upgrade

1. User hits free tier limit (50 photos) or wants unlimited
2. `/trips/[tripId]/upgrade` shows digital (₹399) and print (₹799) tiers
3. Razorpay order created server-side at `/api/payments/create-order`
4. Payment handled by Razorpay checkout JS
5. Razorpay fires webhook to `/api/payments/webhook` (HMAC verified)
6. Webhook is the **sole authoritative write path** for tier upgrades
7. tRPC `upgradeTier` mutation confirms via `webhook_payment_id` stamp (read-only gate)
8. Also supports monthly (₹99) and annual (₹799) subscription plans

## Monetization Strategy

**Freemium (current):**

- Free: 1 trip generation (always free), 50 photos/trip, 500 MB storage, 6 member cap
- Digital tier (₹399 one-time per trip): unlimited photos, unlimited members
- Print tier (₹799 one-time per trip): physical print pack (waitlist at `/api/print-waitlist`)
- Monthly subscription (₹99/month): unlimited trips for the friend group
- Annual subscription (₹799/year): 33% saving vs monthly

**Referral system:** Invite 3 friends → unlock one free generation. Tracked via `profiles.referral_count + referral_bonus_unlocked`.

**Web strategy:** Mobile-first responsive web. No native app. WhatsApp as the primary distribution channel for sharing, not app-store acquisition.
